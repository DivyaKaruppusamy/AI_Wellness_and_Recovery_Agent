SLEEP HYGIENE GUIDE

Adults generally benefit from 7-9 hours of sleep. Maintain a consistent bedtime, reduce screen exposure before bed, avoid heavy meals and excess caffeine late in the day, and keep the bedroom cool, dark, and quiet.

Deep sleep supports physical recovery while REM sleep supports cognitive recovery. If sleep duration or quality is below your normal baseline, consider reducing workout intensity, prioritizing hydration, and allowing extra recovery time. Wearable sleep stages are estimates and should not be treated as clinical measurements.

Low recovery recommendations:
- Light walking
- Mobility exercises
- Stretching
- Earlier bedtime
- Consistent wake time
